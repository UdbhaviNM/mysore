⭐ Must Visit · Palace & Fort

Mysore Palace (Amba Vilas)

The magnificent Mysore Palace is one of the largest palaces in India, and the most visited monument after the Taj Mahal. Built in the Indo-Saracenic style by Henry Irwin in 1912, it features 12 temples, three durbar halls, golden thrones, and stunning stained-glass ceilings. Each Sunday evening it glows with 97,000 bulbs creating an unforgettable spectacle.

📅 Open: 10am–5:30pm Daily 💰 Entry: ₹100 (Adults) ⏱ Duration: 2–3 Hours 🌟 Rating: 4.8/5
⭐ 4.7
Temple · Sacred Hill

Chamundi Hills & Temple

A sacred hill rising 1,065 m above sea level, crowned by the 12th-century Chamundeshwari Temple dedicated to the goddess who slew demon Mahishasura. The 1,000-step stairway passes a 5-m tall Nandi bull statue. The hill offers a panoramic view of Mysore city below.

📍6 km from City Center
⏰7am – 2pm, 3:30pm – 9pm
💰Free Entry
⏱2–4 hrs
⭐ 4.5
Garden · Fountain Show

Brindavan Gardens & KRS Dam

One of India's finest terraced gardens, Brindavan Gardens sprawl across 60 hectares below the Krishnarajasagara (KRS) Dam. The musical fountain show after 6 PM — illuminated in multicolour — draws tens of thousands of visitors every week. Boating is available on the reservoir.

📍19 km from Mysore
⏰6am – 8pm (Fountain 6:30–7:30pm)
💰₹15 Adults, ₹8 Children
⏱2–3 hrs
⭐ 4.6
Wildlife · Zoo

Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens

Established in 1892 under Maharaja Chamaraja Wadiyar X, this 157-ha zoo is one of Asia's oldest and best maintained. Home to gorillas, white tigers, hippopotamus, African lions, and 168+ species. It pioneered open-moat habitats that influenced modern zoo design globally.

📍City Center
⏰8:30am – 5:30pm (Closed Tue)
💰₹100 Adults, ₹50 Children
⏱3–4 hrs
⭐ 4.4
Historical · Island Fort

Srirangapatna

A river island fortress town 16 km from Mysore, once the capital of Hyder Ali and Tipu Sultan. Key sites include Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace (Dariya Daulat Bagh), the Ranganathaswamy Temple, Gumbaz (Tipu's mausoleum), and the fort ramparts with rich British-era cannonball history.

📍16 km from Mysore
⏰9am – 5:30pm Daily
💰₹15 Adults (Museum extra)
⏱Half Day
⭐ 4.7
UNESCO · Hoysala Temple

Somnathpur – Hoysala Temple

The fully preserved Keshava Temple (1268 AD) at Somnathpur is the finest example of Hoysala architecture. Every inch of the outer walls is covered with intricate friezes — elephants, lions, scrolling vines, puranic scenes — in a style found nowhere else on Earth. A UNESCO World Heritage nominee.

📍38 km from Mysore
⏰9am – 5:30pm Daily
💰₹40 Indians, ₹600 Foreigners
⏱2–3 hrs
⭐ 4.3
Palace · Art Gallery

Jaganmohan Palace & Art Gallery

Built in 1861 as a royal residence, this palace now hosts the Jayachamarajendra Art Gallery with rare European oil paintings, Mughal jade artefacts, ancient coins, ivory carvings, and the personal musical instruments of Maharaja Jayachamarajendra Wadiyar — including a priceless pipe organ.

📍Near Mysore Palace
⏰8:30am – 5:30pm Daily
💰₹40 Adults, ₹20 Children
⏱1–2 hrs
⭐ 4.4
Nature Reserve · Bird Watching

Karanji Lake Nature Park

A freshwater lake spanning 90 acres adjacent to the Mysore Zoo, home to 160+ bird species including painted storks, pelicans, cormorants, and migratory flamingos. Features a butterfly park, turtle conservation area, walk-through aviary, and boating. Perfect for morning nature walks.

📍Near Mysore Zoo
⏰8:30am – 5:30pm Daily
💰₹30 Adults, ₹15 Children
⏱1.5–2.5 hrs
⭐ 4.6
Cathedral · Heritage

St. Philomena's Cathedral

One of the largest Gothic-style cathedrals in Asia and the second tallest in India, rising to 54 m. Built between 1933–41, its 700-year-old catacombs hold sacred relics of Saint Philomena. The stained glass windows depicting Biblical scenes are extraordinarily beautiful.

📍City Centre
⏰7am – 6pm (Sundays 7am–7pm)
💰Free Entry
⏱30–60 mins
⭐ 4.2
Museum · Rail Heritage

Mysore Rail Museum

A fascinating open-air museum with rare vintage steam locomotives dating to the 1800s including the horse-drawn Fairy Queen, royal saloon coaches, signal equipment, and the Steam Crane. A joyride on a toy train through the museum grounds is popular with families and railway enthusiasts.

📍Near Mysore Railway Station
⏰10am – 5pm (Closed Mon)
💰₹20 Adults, ₹10 Children
⏱1–2 hrs
Practical Info

Travel Tips for Mysore

Getting Around

Auto-rickshaws and app cabs (Rapido, Ola) are most convenient. Rent a bicycle or scooter to explore at leisure. City buses are cheap but crowded.

Best Season

October–March for ideal weather (22–30°C). October is Dasara – book hotels 3 months in advance. Avoid May–June (40°C+).

Dress Code

Cover shoulders and knees at temples. Remove footwear before entering sacred sites. Loose, breathable cotton clothing is ideal.

Photography

Photography fee at Mysore Palace: ₹50. Night photography (Sunday illumination) is spectacular. No flash inside temples.

Budget Tips

Carry small denominations for auto-rickshaws. Most historical sites have separate Indian/foreigner pricing. Keep your student ID for discounts.

Local Dining

Eat at south Indian darshinis (self-service) for authentic local flavour. Hotel Vinayaka, Mylari, and RRR are legendary local institutions.
