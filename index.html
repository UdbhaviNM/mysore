About Mysuru

The City of Palaces
& Royal Grandeur

Mysore (officially Mysuru) is a magnificent city in the southern Karnataka plateau, celebrated for its majestic palaces, silk weaving, aromatic sandalwood, yoga culture, and the spectacular Dasara festival — hailed as Nadahabba, the state festival of Karnataka.

The city flourished under the Wadiyar dynasty for over 600 years. Today it combines royal heritage with modern energy — featuring premier research institutes, a world-class zoo, lush Chamundi Hills, and vibrant local markets full of incense and colour.

Iconic Highlights
of Mysore

01
Royal Palace

Mysore Palace

The third most visited monument in India. Every Sunday the palace blazes with 97,000 light bulbs — a sight that defines Mysore.

02
Sacred Hill · Temple

Chamundi Hills

A sacred hill 1,065 m above sea level with panoramic views over the entire city and the 12th-century temple at its crown.

03
UNESCO Heritage

Hoysala Temples

The perfectly preserved Keshava Temple at Somnathpur — 38 km from Mysore — is the finest Hoysala architectural masterpiece.

Why Visit

Why Mysore Captivates
Every Traveller

Royal Heritage

600+ years of Wadiyar dynasty rule left breathtaking palaces, forts, and ceremonial grandeur at every corner.

Mysore Silk

The GI-tagged Mysore Crepe Silk — woven from pure mulberry silk with real gold-silver zari — the finest in India.

Yoga Capital

Birthplace of Ashtanga Yoga. Yogis from 80+ countries travel here to study at the world-famous KPJAYI institute.

Brindavan Gardens

60-hectare terraced gardens below KRS Dam with musical fountain show — a romantic marvel after dusk.

Dasara Festival

10 days of elephants, processions, fireworks, and cultural kaleidoscope every October. Karnataka's grandest celebration.

Royal Cuisine

Home to Mysore Pak, Masala Dosa, Bisi Bele Bath — recipes with centuries of royal culinary tradition.

Mysuru Zoo

Established 1892 — one of Asia's finest. Home to white tigers, gorillas, hippos and 168+ species.

Art & Crafts

Mysore Painting, rosewood inlay, sandalwood carving — living art traditions since the 14th century.

Festivals & Celebrations

Mysore celebrates life with colour, music, and grandeur throughout the year.

October

Dasara – Nadahabba

Karnataka's state festival. 10 days of elephant processions, cultural programs, and a spectacular finale at the illuminated palace. Draws over 1 million visitors annually.

October / November

Deepavali

The Festival of Lights transforms Mysore into a shimmering wonderland. The palace illumination reaches its peak, and fireworks light up Chamundi Hills.

February

Varuna Habba – Flower Show

Annual horticultural festival in the Mysore Palace grounds with elaborate floral art, exotic plants, and landscape competitions attracting thousands of enthusiasts.
