Traditional Art Forms
of Mysore

Mysore's cultural wealth is unparalleled in South India. These art forms were nurtured by successive Wadiyar maharajas who were themselves accomplished musicians, painters, and scholars.

Visual Art

Mysore Painting

A classical South Indian painting style developed during the Vijayanagara Empire and refined under Mysore Wadiyars. Distinctive features include delicate brushwork, use of natural pigments, ivory-smooth gesso ground, and 22-karat gold leaf embossed using a stylus. Themes are primarily Hindu deities — Ganesha, Krishna, Saraswati — rendered in graceful, elongated figures with jewel-like detail.

Textile Heritage

Mysore Silk

The GI-tagged Mysore Crepe Silk is woven at the government-owned Karnataka Silk Industries Corporation (KSIC). Pure mulberry silk threads are combined with real gold-silver zari to create the hallmark lustre. Each 6-meter saree takes a master weaver 4–7 days. The Mysore Silk is the official fabric for the robes of the idol at Chamundeshwari Temple.

Classical Dance

Bharatanatyam & Yakshagana

The Mysore gharana of Bharatanatyam is one of the most refined classical dance traditions in India. Yakshagana — a dramatic coastal-Karnataka folk form blending dance, music, dialogue, and elaborate costume — is frequently performed at Dasara and cultural events. Both are actively taught at the Karnataka Lalitha Kala Academy in Mysore.

Classical Music

Carnatic Music

Mysore was a premier centre of Carnatic music under the Wadiyars. Maharaja Jayachamarajendra Wadiyar was a gifted musician who composed over 500 krithis. The city produced legendary musicians including violinist T. Chowdiah, who invented the 7-stringed violin. Annual Sangeetha Sammelana music festivals continue the tradition.

Craft Heritage

Sandalwood Carving

Mysore (and Karnataka at large) is the world's largest producer of Indian sandalwood. Master craftspeople hand-carve intricate figurines, elephants, jewellery boxes, and religious icons from aged sandalwood. The carving process requires 10+ years of training. Government Silk & Sandalwood Showroom (Cauvery Emporium) is the best place to buy authentic pieces.

Folk Craft

Channapatna & Bidri Crafts

Channapatna (45 km from Mysore) is famous for its lacquered wooden toys — colourful, smooth, and non-toxic — made on hand-turned lathes. UNESCO and Geographical Indication recognised. Bidriware (from nearby Bidar) is a distinctive silver-inlay metalwork on zinc-copper alloy with origins in the 15th-century Bahmani courts.

Living Art Form

Mysore Painting –
India's Golden Art

Originating in the Vijayanagara Empire period, Mysore Painting matured under the Wadiyar kings who employed court painters to adorn palace walls, manuscripts, and ritual objects.

The technique involves preparation of a gesso surface (chalk and glue foundation), underdrawing in pencil/ink, applying natural pigments mixed with egg-white binders, and finally pressing 22-karat gold foil for jewellery, halos, and architectural details using a stylus on a wet adhesive base.

Mysore Silk –
Woven in Gold & Light

The most prized silk fabric in India, produced for over 150 years in Mysore under royal and then government patronage.

✓
Pure Mulberry Silk: 100% Bombyx mori silk from Karnataka sericulture farms — the only silk used in royal wedding sarees.
✓
Real Zari: Authentic gold-silver threads (not synthetic) hand-woven into borders and pallu for the distinctive Mysore lustre.
✓
KSRTC Certification: Each piece sold at official KSIC showrooms comes with a certificate guaranteeing authenticity and purity.
✓
Where to Buy: KSIC Showroom on Mananthody Road, Cauvery Emporium in Sayyaji Rao Road — never buy from street vendors.
✓
Price Range: ₹3,000–₹1,50,000+ depending on weight of zari, complexity of design, and purity of silk.
Nadahabba – State Festival

Dasara – The Grand
Festival of Mysore

For 10 magnificent days each October, Mysore transforms into a city of light, colour, and royal pageantry. The Dasara celebration has been held continuously for over 400 years.

10 Days of Dasara

Day 1 – Padyami

Palace Inauguration

The festival begins with a royal Puja in the Mysore Palace. The Goddess Chamundeshwari's idol is ceremonially decorated and the palace opens its private durbar hall.

Days 2–8

Cultural Extravaganza

Seven days of classical music, dance recitals, Yakshagana, wrestling competitions, kalasam (chariot procession), and sports events fill Mysore with energy and colour.

Day 9 – Mahanavami

Royal Durbar & Elephant Puja

The most spectacular day. The Maharaja holds court in full regalia and blesses decorated elephants. 10,000 people fill the palace courtyard. Private durbar is followed by a military parade.

Day 10 – Vijayadashami

The Grand Jamboo Savari Procession

The legendary 7-km golden procession with decorated royal elephants, cavalry, bands, and 100+ floats. Culminates with torch-lit procession at Bannimantap grounds and massive fireworks over Mysore skyline.

Performing Arts of Mysore

Bharatanatyam

The Mysore style (gharana) of Bharatanatyam is known for its pure nritta (rhythmic dance) combined with nritya (expressive dance). The Karnataka Lalitha Kala Academy in Mysore trains hundreds of students annually in this ancient form codified in the Natya Shastra.

Yakshagana

Karnataka's vibrant traditional theatre form — a heady mix of dance, music, spoken dialogue, elaborate costume, and improvisation — performed overnight, traditionally in villages under open skies. Themes are drawn from the Mahabharata, Ramayana, and Puranas. Performances during Dasara are spectacular.

Carnatic Music

Mysore is one of the premier seats of Carnatic classical music. The Wadiyar maharajas didn't merely support music — they participated. Maharaja Jayachamarajendra composed varnams, krithis and keertanas for classical performance. The Sri Vasudevachar Music College continues this 300-year tradition.

Nagaswaram & Percussion

The Nagaswaram (double-reed aerophone) and Tavil (barrel drum) ensemble is an integral part of Mysore temple rituals and processions. Dasara processions feature massive bands of Nadaswara vidwans whose music has remained unchanged for centuries. The palace employs its own traditional musicians.

Kannada Theatre (Nataka)

Mysore has a rich tradition of Kannada-language professional theatre (nagara nataka). T.P. Kailasam, the legendary playwright, was from Mysore. The Rangayana — a repertory theatre company run by the Government of Karnataka in Mysore — is one of India's finest professional drama troupes staging plays year-round.

Dollu Kunitha (Folk Dance)

An energetic folk art performed with large tribal drums (dollu) strapped around the neck. The performers dance vigorously while drumming complex rhythms simultaneously. Primarily performed by the Kuruba community of Karnataka, Dollu Kunitha is a high-energy spectacle at Dasara and rural festivals.

Ashtanga Yoga – Mysore Style

Sri K. Pattabhi Jois founded Ashtanga Vinyasa Yoga in Mysore in 1948 after studying under T. Krishnamacharya — the "father of modern yoga." The "Mysore Style" method — practising independently while a teacher circulates giving adjustments — is now the standard globally. The KPJAYI institute still operates here.

Iyengar Yoga

B.K.S. Iyengar — arguably the world's most influential yoga teacher of the 20th century — was born near Mysore and trained with T. Krishnamacharya. His precision-based system using props is now taught in 80+ countries. The Iyengar Yoga tradition co-exists with Ashtanga in Mysore's thriving yoga community.

Heritage Crafts

Traditional Crafts &

Sandalwood Carving

World-class figurines, jewelry boxes, and architectural pieces hand-carved by master craftsmen taking years to produce each major work.

GI Tag · Karnataka Govt. Protected

Rosewood Inlay

Intricate furniture and decorative items made from rosewood with geometric inlay work using ivory (now synthetic), brass, and silver wire.

Royal Palace Furniture · UNESCO Nominee

Channapatna Toys

Lacquered wooden toys from Channapatna town known as "Toy Town of India" – uses vegetable dyes and hand-turned craft, exported worldwide.

GI Tag · Toy Town of Karnataka

Mysore Agarbatti (Incense)

Mysore is India's incense capital, producing 80% of the country's agarbatti. Made with sandalwood paste, essential oils, charcoal, and Jigat binder.

India's Incense Capital

Mysore Painting Workshop

Several workshops in Mysore offer 1–7 day courses to learn the technique of Mysore painting, from gesso preparation to gold leafing. Chitrakala Parishad is the best.

UNESCO Living Heritage Form

Bronze Casting (Dhokra)

The lost-wax casting method producing bronze figurines of Hindu deities, ritual vessels, and decorative objects — a tradition over 4,000 years old in south India.
