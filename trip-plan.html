When to Go

Best Time to
Visit Mysore

Spring
MARCH – MAY

Hot and dry. Good for offseason pricing and less crowds. Morning visits only advised. 28°C–40°C.

⭐⭐⭐
Monsoon
JUNE – SEPT

Lush green landscapes. Gardens and parks are beautiful. Occasional heavy rain. 22°C–30°C.

⭐⭐⭐⭐
Autumn ★ BEST
OCTOBER – NOV

Dasara in October! Perfect weather. 22°C–30°C. The most magical season to visit Mysore.

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Winter
DEC – FEB

Pleasant and cool. Ideal for outdoor sightseeing. Nights can be chilly (15°C). Most comfortable season.

Day-by-Day Plans

Mysore Itineraries

Choose your trip length and explore our curated day-by-day plans crafted by local experts.

Day 1

The Essential Mysore Experience

Palace · Chamundi Hills · Zoo · Devaraja Market · Palace Lighting

7:00 AM

Filter Coffee & Breakfast at Mylari Hotel

Kick off with Mysore's most famous dosas and aromatic filter coffee. Arrive early — the queue starts at 6:30 AM and they close by 11 AM. Order the neer dosa and coconut chutney combo.

💰 Budget: ₹60–100/person 📍 Nazarbad
9:00 AM

Mysore Palace (Amba Vilas)

Explore the palace's 12 temples, golden-domed durbar halls, and the royal armoury and gemstone collection. Audio guides are highly recommended. Check the palace tour schedule for guided walkthroughs in English.

💰 ₹100/adult ⏱ 2–3 hours 📷 Photography ₹50 extra
12:00 PM

Devaraja Market

A riot of colour — this century-old market overflows with garlands of jasmine, heaps of turmeric, stalls of Mysore silk, incense, sandalwood soap, and local fruits. Don't leave without buying premium Mysore agarbatti (incense).

🛒 Free to Browse 🛍️ Best for souvenirs
1:30 PM

Lunch at Hotel Dasaprakash

Enjoy a full Karnataka vegetarian thali with unlimited rice, sambar, rasam, kootu, papad, and payasam for dessert. One of Mysore's most celebrated restaurants for genuine local fare since 1956.

💰 ₹120–200/person ⏱ 45 mins
3:00 PM

Chamundi Hills

Drive up to the Chamundeshwari Temple, or for the adventurous, climb the 1,008 steps. Don't miss the colossal Nandi bull statue halfway up. Views of the Mysore city spread below are spectacular in the afternoon light.

Free entry ⏱ 1.5 hrs 👗 Cover legs & shoulders
6:00 PM

Evening Snack: Churmuri & Street Food

Head to Lansdowne Building area for evening street food — churmuri, mirchi bajji, corn, and local chaat. Sip on seasonal fresh juice from stalls near Statue Circle or Gandhi Square.

💰 ₹20–100 🌇 Golden hour views
7:00 PM

✨ Palace Illumination (Sunday Only)

Every Sunday (7–7:30 PM), the Mysore Palace is lit with 97,000 bulbs — a sight that defines Mysore for most visitors. Arrive 30 mins early for a good spot on the viewing lawns. For weekday visits, the interior lighting is still spectacular at night.

⭐ Must-see highlight 📷 Perfect for photography
Day 1

Palace, Hills & Culture

Palace · Market · Chamundi · Jaganmohan Art Gallery · Palace Night

7:30 AM

Breakfast at Mylari + KSIC Silk Showroom

After breakfast, head to the KSIC Silk Showroom to browse (and possibly buy) authentic Mysore Silk sarees with GI tag certification. The weaving demonstration is educational and free.

10:00 AM

Mysore Palace (Full Tour)

Take the complete 3-hour palace experience including the audio guide, all 5 durbar halls, the royal throne room, and the picture gallery with portraits of all 24 Wadiyar rulers.

2:00 PM

Chamundi Hills + Karanji Lake

Visit Chamundi Hills temple and then descend to Karanji Lake Nature Park for bird watching and a walk in the butterfly enclosure. Flamingos and painted storks visible from October–February.

5:00 PM

Jaganmohan Palace Art Gallery

See the jade artefacts, European oil paintings, rare musical instruments, and ivory exhibition in this lesser-known but extraordinary royal museum just 5 minutes from the main palace.

7:30 PM

Dinner at Green Hotel Restaurant

Heritage dining at its finest. Karnataka home cuisine in a 1920s property. Try the local thali or Bisi Bele Bath. Evening cultural performances occasionally happen in the courtyard.

Day 2

Day Trips: Srirangapatna & Brindavan

Fort Island · Tipu's Palace · KRS Dam · Musical Fountain

8:00 AM

Srirangapatna Island Visit

16 km from Mysore. Explore Tipu Sultan's fortress, Dariya Daulat Bagh palace museum, Gumbaz mausoleum, and the Ranganathaswamy riverside temple. Hire a local guide (₹200) for rich historical context.

⏱ 3–4 hrs 🏛️ Rich history
1:00 PM

Lunch at KRS Dam Area

Several restaurants near KRS Dam serve decent Karnataka fare. Pre-book at Mysore Ruchi for better quality. Or carry a packed lunch from Mysore city.

2:30 PM

Brindavan Gardens & KRS Dam

Walk the terraced gardens, take a boat ride on the reservoir, and enjoy the lush greenery. Make sure you're there for the musical fountain show at 6:30 PM (entry separate for the fountain viewing area).

🎶 Fountain: 6:30–7:30 PM 💰 ₹15 entry
8:30 PM

Return to Mysore – Shop for Souvenirs

Sayyaji Rao Road is lined with government emporiums. Buy: Mysore Pak from Sri Krishna Sweets, sandalwood products from Cauvery Emporium, agarbatti from any market stall.

Where to Stay

Best Hotels in Mysore

From royal heritage suites to budget-friendly guesthouses — options for every kind of traveller.

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Lalitha Mahal Palace Hotel

📍 Mysore Palace, White Rock, Mysore

The former guest palace of the Wadiyar maharajas, now managed by ITDC. An architectural masterpiece with a dramatic staircase, billiard room, rooftop swimming pool, and impeccable old-world service. The most iconic place to stay in Mysore.

₹8,000 / night onwards
⭐⭐⭐⭐

Royal Orchid Metropole

📍 Jhansi Lakshmi Bai Road, Mysore

A stately 1920 heritage property that was the British officers' guest house. The pool, bar, and restaurant are excellent. Period furniture, lush gardens, and attentive staff. Walking distance to the palace and main market.

₹4,500 / night onwards
⭐⭐⭐⭐

The Windflower Prakruthi Resort

📍 Bogadi, Mysore

A serene eco-resort with traditional mud cottages, Ayurvedic spa, organic farm, yoga pavilion, and swimming tank. Perfect for travellers seeking wellness and nature alongside Mysore's cultural highlights.

₹5,500 / night onwards
⭐⭐⭐

The Green Hotel

📍 Vinoba Road, Mysore

A charming budget-luxury option in a 1920s heritage bungalow. Filled with antiques, heritage charm, and a beautiful garden. The eco-friendly ethos means all profits go to local charities. Excellent homemade food.

₹2,000 / night onwards
⭐⭐

Hotel Mayura Hoysala

📍 2 Jhansi Lakshmi Bai Road, Mysore

A government-run KSTDC hotel with clean rooms, good value for money, close to the palace. Very popular with domestic travelers for its location, reliability, and simple but delicious Karnataka-style restaurant.

₹1,200 / night onwards
🛏️

Zostel Mysore

📍 Nazarbad, Mysore

Mysore's best backpacker hostel with social common areas, clean dorms, private rooms, and excellent travel planning support. Great community of international backpackers. Walking distance to food and nightlife zones.

₹400 / dorm bed · ₹1,200 private
Get There & Around

Getting To & Around Mysore

✈️

By Air

Mysore Airport (MYQ) has limited flights from Bengaluru, Chennai, and Delhi. Kempegowda International Airport (BLR) in Bengaluru is 170 km away. Take KSRTC AC bus or hire a cab to Mysore from BLR Airport (₹1,800–2,500).

🚂

By Train

Mysore Railway Station is connected to Bengaluru (Shatabdi – 2 hrs), Chennai (Chamundi Express – 8 hrs), and Mumbai (Kaveri Express – 24 hrs). Book 2–3 weeks in advance on IRCTC. Mysore is well-connected by rail.

🚌

By Bus

KSRTC runs frequent buses from Bengaluru (3 hrs, ₹150–600 AC), Ooty (3.5 hrs), Coorg (3 hrs), and all major Karnataka cities. Mysore's KSRTC bus stand is the hub for all intercity connections.

🛺

Auto Rickshaw

The most fun way around Mysore's compact city. Negotiate a half-day or full-day rate (₹500–800) with a driver-guide who knows the sites. Most auto drivers speak basic English. Meter autos also available.

🚗

Cab / Self Drive

Ola and Rapido work well in Mysore. For day trips to Srirangapatna, Somnathpur, and Brindavan, hire a cab for the day (₹1,500–2,500). Self-drive rentals from Zoomcar or Drivezy are available.

🚲

Cycling

Mysore is surprisingly cycle-friendly within the palace area. Several workshops near Gandhi Square rent bicycles (₹50–100/day). Dedicated cycle lanes near the zoo and palace grounds make it safe and scenic.
