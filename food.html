Mysore Cuisine

Mysore Special Food

A culinary kingdom of rich spices, royal recipes, golden sweets, and centuries-old traditions passed through generations of master cooks.

Scroll
Signature Flavours

The Legendary Tastes
of Mysore

Mysore's food culture blends royal court recipes with everyday street food brilliance. Here are the iconic dishes every visitor must taste.

🏆 Most Iconic · Sweet

Mysore Pak

Created accidentally in 1935 in the kitchens of the Mysore Palace by royal chef Kakasura Madappa for Maharaja Krishnaraja Wadiyar IV, Mysore Pak is a dense, ghee-soaked sweet made from besan (gram flour), pure ghee, and sugar. The palace version is crumbly with a melt-in-mouth texture unlike the softer commercial versions.

🧈 Pure Ghee 🫘 Besan Flour 🍬 Sugar Syrup 🌰 Cardamom
🏠 Must Buy: Sri Krishna Sweets 💰 ₹30–50 per piece
🟢
⭐ 4.9
Breakfast / Brunch

Mysore Masala Dosa

The legendary crispy rice crepe with a generous smear of spicy Mysore chutney (made from dried red chilies, garlic, and roasted chana dal), stuffed with spiced potato masala. The distinctive redness of the chutney inside is what makes a Mysore Masala Dosa different from any other dosa in the world.

🍽️ Breakfast staple 💰 ₹60–120 🌶️ Medium spicy
🟢
⭐ 4.8
Main Course

Bisi Bele Bath

Literally "hot lentil rice dish." A royal Karnataka classic — a wholesome one-pot dish of rice, toor dal, mixed vegetables, tamarind, and an aromatic spice mix (Bisi Bele Bath powder) with cloves, cinnamon, and dried coconut. Served piping hot with ghee on top and boondi or papad on the side.

🍽️ Lunch / Dinner 💰 ₹80–150 ♨️ Served hot
🟢
⭐ 4.7
Royal Sweet

Chiroti (Chirote)

A royal Karnataka and Maharashtra pastry — thin, layered sheets of deep-fried dough dusted with fine sugar powder and cardamom. Originally served at Mysore royal banquets, Chiroti is melt-in-mouth, flaky, and faintly sweet. Often dipped in almond milk (badam milk) for a richer experience.

🏰 Royal origin 💰 ₹20–40 per piece 🎊 Festival special
🟢
⭐ 4.6
Breakfast / Snack

Batata Vada & Idli Vada

Soft, fluffy idlis paired with crispy, golden medu vadas (made from urad dal) served with coconut chutney and sambar. Mysore's darshinis (eateries) serve these fresh from 7–11 AM. The local twist is the uniquely aromatic sambar with curry leaves and special masalas unique to each restaurant.

🍽️ Breakfast classic 💰 ₹40–80 🌿 Vegan-friendly
🟢
⭐ 4.7
Soup / Side Dish

Mysore Rasam

A thin, tangy, peppery broth made from tamarind juice, tomatoes, toor dal water, and Mysore rasam powder — a fiery blend of dried red chilies, coriander, and cumin roasted with asafoetida. Sipped as soup or mixed with rice. The Mysore Rasam is spicier and more aromatic than Tamil Nadu versions.

🍽️ Lunch / Dinner 💰 ₹30–60 🌶️ Spicy & Tangy
🟢
⭐ 4.5
Street Food / Snack

Churmuri (Puffed Rice Chaat)

Mysore's quintessential street snack — puffed rice tossed with freshly grated coconut, onions, tomatoes, green chilies, coriander leaves, lime juice, and a drizzle of coconut oil served in a newspaper cone. An evening institution in Mysore streets and Devaraja Market area.

🛒 Street food 💰 ₹15–30 ⚡ Quick snack
🟢
⭐ 4.6
Sweet / Dessert

Rave Kesari (Semolina Halwa)

A bright orange semolina (sooji/rava) dessert scented with saffron and cardamom, studded with cashews and raisins fried in golden ghee. In Mysore, Kesari is a temple prasad and a breakfast-table sweet. Its distinctive deep saffron colour comes from generous use of natural saffron or food-grade colour.

🛕 Temple prasad 💰 ₹25–60 🌼 Saffron perfumed
🟢
⭐ 5.0
Beverage · South Indian

Mysore Filter Coffee

The lifeblood of Mysore mornings. Coffee decoction (from a brass filter) mixed with frothy hot milk, served in a traditional stainless steel tumbler-and-davara set. Mysore Coffee blends Arabica from nearby Coorg estates with chicory for a rich, slightly bitter, aromatic cup unlike any café coffee.

☕ Morning ritual 💰 ₹15–30 🌿 Coorg Arabica
🟢
⭐ 4.5
Sweet · Indian Fudge

Coconut Burfi & Kaju Katli

Mysore is renowned for its mithai shops. Coconut Burfi (from fresh grated coconut, sugar, and cardamom) and Kaju Katli (cashew paste and sugar, silver-leafed) are Mysore specialties. Every sweet shop in Devaraja Market area has its own centuries-old recipe passed down through families.

🥥 Coconut rich 💰 ₹60–200/100g 🎁 Great souvenir

Make at Home

Recipe: Mysore Pak

The legendary royal sweet you can recreate at home with just 3 ingredients.

  • 2 cups Besan (gram flour), sifted
  • 2.5 cups Pure Desi Ghee
  • 2 cups Sugar
  • ½ cup Water
  • ¼ tsp Cardamom powder
  • A pinch of Baking soda (optional for porous texture)
  • Heat ghee in a heavy pan and fry besan on low flame till raw smell disappears (15 min).
  • Dissolve sugar in water and cook to a single-thread consistency syrup.
  • Slowly pour besan-ghee mixture into the syrup while stirring vigorously.
  • Cook until the mixture starts leaving the sides of the pan and looks foamy.
  • Add cardamom, pour into a greased tray, cool 10 mins, cut into pieces.

Use only pure ghee (not dalda or vanaspati) for authentic flavour. The secret to palace-style Mysore Pak is generous ghee — don't reduce it. Cut while warm, not hot. Store in an airtight container for up to 2 weeks.

"When I tasted Mysore Pak for the first time at the palace, I understood why food can be royal."

— Food Historian, Karnataka
Where to Eat

Best Restaurants
& Food Spots

🍽️

Mylari Hotel

Nazarbad, Mysore

The most famous Dosa restaurant in Mysore, open since 1936. Their neer dosa and masala dosa are legendary. Very limited menu, cash only, long morning queues — worth every minute.

⭐ 4.9
🍰

Sri Krishna Sweets

📍 Sayyaji Rao Road, Mysore

The best Mysore Pak in the city, period. This shop has been making the original crumbly version since the 1950s. Also excellent for chiroti, rave kesari, and kaju katli. Buy a kilo to take home.

⭐ 4.8
🍛

Hotel Dasaprakash

📍 Gandhi Square, Mysore

A Mysore institution since 1956. Known for the thali (full South Indian meals) with unlimited rice, sambar, rasam, curries, kootu, payasam. Excellent value for a royal spread at budget prices.

⭐ 4.7
☕

Café Coffee Day (MG Road)

📍 MG Road & Devaraja Market area

While the legacy filter coffee at local darshinis is the real experience, note that CCD was founded in Chikmagalur, very close to Mysore. The local filter coffee at darshinis beats any café version hands down.

⭐ 4.3
🍃

Green Hotel Restaurant

📍 Vinoba Road Heritage Hotel, Mysore

Situated in a gorgeous heritage building, this eco-friendly restaurant serves authentic Karnataka home food. Profits support social welfare programs in the region. Try the Bisi Bele Bath and local pickles.

⭐ 4.6
🌙

RRR Restaurant

📍 Gandhi Square, Mysore

Famous for non-vegetarian Andhra-style meals. RRR's signature is unlimited rice thali with mutton curry, chicken fry, and a dozen different chutneys. Popular with locals and tourists alike for hearty evening meals.

⭐ 4.5
